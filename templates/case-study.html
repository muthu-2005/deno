Usain Bolt, the Jamaican sprinter renowned for his world records in the 100m and 200m events, faced a setback when his teammate, Nesta Carter, tested positive for a banned substance following the 2008 Beijing Olympics. As a result, Bolt's 4x100m relay team was disqualified, stripping him of one of his nine Olympic gold medals.

At Rest (Baseline Level) 30–50 20–40
During Intense Training 60–80 40–60

In 1997, tennis legend Andre Agassi tested positive for methamphetamine during a routine drug test. Following an investigation by the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) and the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP), he was handed a three-month suspension from professional tennis. Despite this setback, Agassi returned to reclaim his place in tennis history, becoming one of the sport's all-time greats.
